Power Supply Components

Power is the lifeline of any robot. Without stable power, a robot cannot operate properly.

  • Battery ‚Äì Provides energy to the entire robot. Types include:
  • Li-Po (Lithium-Polymer): High energy density, lightweight, commonly used in drones and humanoids.
  • Li-Ion (Lithium-Ion): Rechargeable, stable, ideal for mobile robots.
  • NiMH (Nickel Metal Hydride): Safer and cheaper, but heavier.
  • Voltage Regulators ‚Äì Ensure the robot circuits receive constant voltage. For example, LM7805 gives stable 5V output to sensors and microcontrollers.
  • DC-DC Converters ‚Äì Step up or step down voltage to power different components (like motors needing 12V while sensors need 5V).
  • Power Switches / Relays ‚Äì Enable safe control of power, turning devices on/off remotely or automatically.

Microcontrollers & Processing Units

Microcontrollers are the ‚Äúbrain‚Äù of your robot. They read sensors, process data, and control actuators.

  • Arduino (Uno, Mega, Nano): Easy to program with C/C++. Ideal for beginners.
  • Raspberry Pi: Mini computer with Linux OS. Perfect for AI, vision processing, and advanced robots.
  • ESP32 / ESP8266: Microcontrollers with built-in Wi-Fi and Bluetooth for IoT and remote-controlled robots.
  • STM32 / PIC Microcontrollers: Advanced microcontrollers for precise and high-speed robotic tasks.

Sensors

Sensors allow robots to ‚Äúsense‚Äù their environment, making them intelligent.

  • Ultrasonic Sensor (HC-SR04): Uses sound waves to measure distance. Useful in obstacle-avoiding robots.
  • IR Sensor / IR Proximity Sensor: Detects nearby objects and lines; commonly used in line-following robots.
  • Temperature Sensor (LM35, DHT11/22): Measures heat; can control robot fans or monitor environment.
  • Gyroscope & Accelerometer (MPU6050): Detect orientation and movement; essential for humanoids to balance.
  • Light Sensor / LDR: Measures light intensity; used in solar robots or light-following robots.
  • Touch / Force Sensor: Detects physical contact; used in robotic arms and human-interactive robots.
  • Camera Module (Raspberry Pi Camera, OV7670): Provides vision for object detection and recognition.

Actuators

Actuators are what make robots move. They convert electrical energy into mechanical motion.

  • DC Motors: Provide continuous rotation. Common in wheeled robots.
  • Servo Motors: Provide precise angular movement. Used in robot arms and humanoid joints.
  • Stepper Motors: Move in steps, allowing very precise positioning. Used in 3D printers and CNC robots.
  • Solenoids: Create linear motion; often used for robotic grippers or locks.
  • Linear Actuators: Extend/retract parts of robots, like lifting arms or opening doors.

üí° Tip: For humanoid robots, servo motors are critical for walking and arm movement due to precision control.

Driver & Control Circuits

Microcontrollers cannot usually supply enough power for motors. Driver circuits help with that.

  • Motor Driver ICs (L298N, L293D): Control speed and direction of DC and stepper motors.
  • H-Bridge Circuits: Allow motors to rotate forward or backward.
  • Relay Modules: Switch high-power devices using low-power control signals.

Passive Components

Basic building blocks of electronics.

  • Resistors: Control the flow of current, protect components.
  • Capacitors: Store energy, smooth voltage fluctuations.
  • Diodes / LEDs: Control current direction, light indicators.
  • Transistors: Switch circuits or amplify signals.

Other Useful Modules

Special components for advanced robots.

  • IMU Sensor: Combines gyroscope, accelerometer, and magnetometer for precise motion tracking.
  • RFID Module: Identifies objects or people, used in access-control robots.
  • GPS Module: Navigation and location tracking for autonomous robots.

Applications

  • Manufacturing: Assembly lines, welding, packaging.
  • Healthcare: Surgery, rehabilitation, eldercare.
  • Exploration: Space, underwater, hazardous environments.
  • Service: Delivery robots, cleaning robots, hospitality robots.
  • Education: Teaching programming, AI, and mechanical concepts.
