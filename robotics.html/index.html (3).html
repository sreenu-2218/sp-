Drones & Technology

Introduction

Drone technology (also called UAV – Unmanned Aerial Vehicle) is a major branch of Robotics and Automation where robots are designed to fly autonomously or semi-autonomously using sensors, microcontrollers, communication systems, and AI.

A drone = robot + flying mechanism.

Drones are used in:

  • Agriculture
  • Defense & surveillance
  • Disaster management
  • Delivery systems
  • Filmmaking
  • Mapping & 3D modelling
  • Industrial inspections

    • Drones combine embedded systems, electronics, mechanical design, and AI, making them an important part of modern robotics.

    Frame & Aerodynamics

    The frame is the physical body of the drone, designed for strength and lightweight performance.

    • Made using carbon fiber, plastic, or aluminum
    • Must resist vibrations
    • Aerodynamics decide lift, drag, and stability
    • Quadcopter (X-shape, +-shape)
    • Hexacopter
    • Octocopter
    • Fixed-wing

    Motors & Propulsion System

      What it includes:

    • Brushless DC motors (BLDC)
    • Propellers
    • Electronic Speed Controller (ESC)

      • Function:

      Motors generate thrust. ESC controls motor speed. Propellers convert rotational motion into lift.

    • KV rating (rpm per volt)
    • Thrust-to-weight ratio
    • Propeller size & pitch

    Flight Controller (The Brain)

    What it is:

    A microcontroller-based board that processes sensor data and stabilizes the drone.

    • Pixhawk
    • ArduPilot
    • DJI Naza
    • KK2.1

      • Role

    • Reads sensors (IMU, GPS)
    • Balances the drone
    • Runs navigation algorithms
    • Communicates with remote controller

    Sensors in Drones

    Drones use a combination of sensors:

    IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit)

    Contains:

    • Accelerometer
    • Gyroscope
    • Magnetometer

      • Used for orientation, angle, and rotation detection.

      GPS Module

      Used for:

      • Position estimation
      • Path planning
      • Return-to-Home

      Return-to-Home

      Measures altitude using air pressure.

      Ultrasonic/ LiDAR sensors

      Used for obstacle detection and landing.

      Optical Flow Sensor

      Detects motion relative to ground.

    Power System

    Components

    • Li-Po battery (3S, 4S, 6S)
    • ESCs
    • Voltage/Current sensor

    Why Li-Po?

    High discharge rate → supports high motor speed.

    Drone Power Concepts

    • C-rating
    • Flight time calculation
    • Battery management

    Communication Systems

    Drones communicate through:

    RC Transmitter/Receiver

    Range: 1–2 km

    Used for manual control

    Telemetry Modules (433MHz/915MHz)

    Used for live data to ground station (Mission Planner).

    Wi-Fi / 4G / 5G

    Used in autonomous drones for:

    • Live streaming
    • Cloud-based control
    • AI processing

    Navigation & Control Algorithms

    Stabilization & PID Control

    PID = Proportional + Integral + Derivative

    Used to stabilize:

  • Roll
  • Pitch
  • Yaw
    •

    If PID tuning is poor → drone shakes or drifts.
