Humanoid Arm

Introduction to Humanoid Arm

A Humanoid Arm is a robotic arm designed to imitate the structure and movement of a human arm. It consists of joints, links, motors, and control systems that work together to perform actions such as lifting, gripping, rotating, and placing objects. Humanoid arms are widely used in robotics research, automation, prosthetics, and humanoid robot development because they help in understanding human-like motion and manipulation.

Mechanical Structure (Links and Joints)

The humanoid arm is made up of rigid links connected by joints, similar to the shoulder, elbow, and wrist in a human arm. These joints allow rotational or angular movement, enabling flexible motion.

Microcontroller

  • A microcontroller (such as Arduino, PIC, or ESP32) acts as the brain of the robot. It reads sensor data, processes it using a programmed logic, and sends control signals to the motors to follow the line accurately.

Actuators (Servo Motors)

  • Servo motors are commonly used as actuators in humanoid arms. They provide precise control over angle and position, making them suitable for joint movements such as bending and rotating the arm.

Motor Driver / Control Interface

  • In cases where high-power motors are used, motor drivers or control circuits are required. For servo motors, PWM (Pulse Width Modulation) signals from the microcontroller are sufficient to control position and speed.

End Effector (Gripper or Hand)

  • The end effector is the part of the arm that interacts with objects. It can be a simple gripper or a more advanced robotic hand with fingers, designed to hold, release, or manipulate objects.
