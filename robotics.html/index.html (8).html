Robotics Fundamentals

Introduction

Robotics is the interdisciplinary branch of engineering and science that includes mechanical, electrical, and computer engineering. Robotics focuses on designing, constructing, operating, and using robots.

Modern robots are used in industrial automation, healthcare, military applications, exploration, and service tasks. The field combines mechanics, electronics, computer science, and artificial intelligence.

Types of Robots

Robots are classified based on structure, motion, application, and autonomy.

  • Industrial Robots: Used in factories for welding, painting, assembly, and packaging.
  • Service Robots: Assist humans in tasks like cleaning, delivery, or customer interaction.
  • Humanoid Robots: Robots that resemble human form and behavior, often used for research or service.
  • Autonomous Robots: Operate independently using sensors and AI, e.g., self-driving cars.
  • Exploration Robots: Used in hazardous or remote locations, such as space or underwater.

Components

  • Mechanical Structure: The frame and body that form the robot’s shape and support.
  • Actuators: Motors and devices that produce movement.
  • Sensors: Detect environmental inputs like distance, light, or temperature.
  • Control System: The “brain” of the robot, processing inputs and sending commands.
  • Power Supply: Batteries or external power that drive actuators and electronics.

Sensors

Sensors allow robots to perceive and respond to the environment.

  • Proximity Sensors: Detect nearby objects using infrared, ultrasonic, or capacitive methods.
  • Vision Sensors: Cameras and image processing to recognize objects, people, or patterns.
  • Force Sensors: Measure physical contact or torque for manipulation tasks.
  • Temperature Sensors: Monitor heat for safety or process control.
  • Gyroscopes and Accelerometers: Detect orientation, balance, and motion.

Actuators

Actuators are responsible for moving parts of a robot.

  • Electric Motors: Convert electrical energy into rotational or linear motion.
  • Hydraulic Actuators: Use pressurized fluid for high-force applications.
  • Pneumatic Actuators: Use compressed air for light and fast movements.

Control Systems

Control systems manage robot behavior and movement:

  • Open-Loop Control: Actions occur without feedback; simple but less accurate.
  • Closed-Loop Control: Feedback is used to adjust actions for precision and stability.
  • AI-Based Control: Robots learn and adapt behavior using artificial intelligence.

Kinematics

Kinematics studies motion without considering forces.

  • Forward Kinematics: Determines end-effector position based on joint angles.
  • Inverse Kinematics: Calculates joint angles needed to reach a desired position.

Dynamics

Dynamics considers forces and torques that produce motion.

It’s crucial for accurate motion, stability, and predicting the behavior of robots under load.

Programming

Robots are programmed to perform tasks autonomously.

  • Languages: Python, C++, Java, MATLAB.
  • Frameworks: ROS (Robot Operating System), V-REP, Gazebo for simulation.
  • Algorithms: Path planning, motion control, AI, computer vision.

Applications

  • Manufacturing: Assembly lines, welding, packaging.
  • Healthcare: Surgery, rehabilitation, eldercare.
  • Exploration: Space, underwater, hazardous environments.
  • Service: Delivery robots, cleaning robots, hospitality robots.
  • Education: Teaching programming, AI, and mechanical concepts.
